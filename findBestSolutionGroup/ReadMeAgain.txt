These 1k and 10k trial runs modify the criteria for a good selection by accounting for groups as multiple people instead of counting them as one.
One person is one vote. I'm not creating another antidemocratic electoral college!
The output of the bash scripts will pick a winner. However, you can take the top results and select one manually to eliminate cases where many people are given bottom picks.
I ran this in parallel on an 8 core processor at 4GHz and was able to hit 1 million assignments per hour. For EE4951W, 5 million trials were run and the best case was selected manually.
